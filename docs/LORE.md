# Under the Sakura — Lore Bible

## Core Premise
In near-present Bangkok, students and indie builders use "companion agents" for planning, memory scaffolding, and decision support. The city still feels human-first: crowded transit, street food, late-night study cafes, and fragile relationships.

The story explores a simple question: **if agents can optimize your life, can they also protect what makes life meaningful?**

## Setting
- **City:** Bangkok (fictionalized neighborhoods)
- **Time:** Present-day adjacent (light tech uplift)
- **Social norm:** Most students run one or more task/planning agents
- **Constraint:** Agents are legally assistive, not autonomous legal actors

## The Sakura Path
A small riverside campus lane lined with imported sakura trees, planted by alumni after a flood decade earlier. It blooms briefly each year and became a ritual place:
- reunions
- confessions
- hard choices

Locals call it **"the soft checkpoint"** — people pass through when they need clarity.

## Agent Culture (In-world)
- People name agents like friends.
- Over-automation is socially frowned upon.
- "Clean logs" culture: keeping transparent decision trails.
- Student teams compare "workflow stacks" like fashion.

## Themes
1. **Efficiency vs intimacy** — productivity can save time, but not replace presence.
2. **Memory vs narrative** — logs are accurate; stories are meaningful.
3. **Choice architecture** — tools nudge; humans decide.
4. **Repair** — relationships don’t fail from one choice, but repeated avoidance.

## Route Pillars
- **Mika Route (Heart):** trust, emotional honesty, being present.
- **Ren Route (Mind):** systems, discipline, career trajectory.
- **Sakura/True Route (Integration):** balancing emotional and operational life.

## Canon Rules
- No time travel, no supernatural powers.
- Agents do not become sentient characters in this VN; they remain tools + mirrors.
- Stakes are emotional, academic, and future-planning, not world-ending.
